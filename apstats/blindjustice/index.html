Justice: Is It Colorbl12nd?
An expository article on Race and the Death Penalty

        Is our justice system really just? In 1998, the Death Penalty Information Center published a study that examined the number of defendants convicted of murder that were sentenced to death in the Philadelphia court system, between 1983 and 1993. The tables below represent their findings, the upper displaying the number of convicts executed, the lower showing the percentage.

Black Victim White Victim Total
Black Defendant 76 of 422 21 of 99 97 of 521
White Defendant 1 of 25 17 of 121 18 of 146
Total 77 of 447 38 of 220 115 of 667
Black Victim White Victim Total
Black Defendant 18.0% 21.2% 18.6%
White Defendant 4.0% 14.1% 12.3%
Total 17.2% 17.3% 17.2%

        The data shows that about the same percentage of convicts were executed for murder of a black or white victim, however, when looking at the executions in the context of the defendants, a discrepancy becomes apparent. Nearly 6.3% more black defendants are executed for homicide. Further analysis of the DPIC's findings show even more troubling evidence.

Sentences of Black and White defendants for homicides

        Approximately 18% of black defendants were executed for the murder of a black victim. The percentage jumps to 21.2% when the victim was white. Looking at the cases of white defendants, 14.1% were executed for killing a white victim. The number plummets to 4% when the victim was black.

Sentences of Black Defendants for Homicides

        Approximately 18% of black defendants were executed for the murder of a black victim. The percentage jumps to 21.2% when the victim was white. Looking at the cases of white defendants, 14.1% were executed for killing a white victim. The number plummets to 4% when the victim was black.
